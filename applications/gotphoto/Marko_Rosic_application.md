## Verdict: Good Fit (Strong on paper, dragged to Good by culture)

Skills/role/visa alignment is genuinely strong — one of the better-matched, clearly-sponsoring leadership roles surfaced so far. But the culture/stability red flags are **amplified by the relocation + visa lock-in**: a sponsored visa ties him to this employer, so a chaotic org with 7-manager churn is far riskier here than for a local hire who can leave freely.

### Key strengths
- "Build and shape a young design department into a larger organization" = exactly what he did at Gentoo (company-level design system, team of 7, two team leads).
- Design systems / typography / spacing / A/B testing / user research map to primary skills.
- Clean sector pivot out of gambling; explicit visa + relocation support; Germany primary target.

### Gaps / risks
- 🚩 **Culture & stability** (Berlin 2.0/5, 7 managers, EQT pressure) — the deciding concern given visa lock-in.
- **Working language** unstated — no German; confirm English is the working language.
- **Visa specifics** — confirm sponsorship covers a non-EU Serbian national, not just EU-internal relocation.
- **Altitude** — "Lead" reporting to Head of Product, no design exec above; design has no senior advocate, compounding the instability risk.
- Minor: motion design (nice-to-have), e-commerce depth — framable around consumer-web + conversion work.

### Recommendation
**Apply — eyes open, not top priority over a healthier-culture sponsor.** Make diligence the gating step, not the CV. In screening, ask pointedly about the management churn, the design team's current size/mandate, and what stability looks like under EQT. Confirm working language + visa-for-Serbian-nationals **before** investing heavily. If answers are weak, downgrade to backup.

### Pre-application diligence (confirm before heavy investment)
1. Is the working language English?
2. Current design headcount + the concrete growth plan for the department?
3. Does visa sponsorship apply to a non-EU **Serbian** citizen?
